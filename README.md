## Aim

To define UX problem statements, select appropriate research methods, and create user personas for both a Public Transport Application and a Food Donation Application to solve core user pain points.



# Scenario 1: Designing a Public Transport App for Daily Commuters



### 1. UX Problem Statements



* **User Pain Points:** Unpredictable arrival times, lack of real-time updates, and complex navigation interfaces.


* **Problem Statement 1:** *"A daily commuter needs a way to view real-time bus/train locations because fixed schedules often don't reflect delays, which results in missed rides and extended wait times."*

* **Problem Statement 2:** *"A first-time traveler needs a way to navigate unfamiliar routes easily because the app lacks clear, guided steps, which results in stress and confusion during the journey."*


### 2. Research Methods & Justification



* **Field Observations:** To watch how people interact with apps in transit environments. Uncovers real-time context and distractions.


* **Interviews:** To collect specific needs and pain points. Provides clear habit insights.


* **Usability Testing (on-the-go):** To evaluate live interactions while commuting. Ensures practical usability.



### 3. Personas



* **Persona 1 (Rahul Sen):** 29, Male, Delhi, Software Developer. Uses metro daily. Goal is to reach work on time with minimal transfers. Pain point is inaccurate ETAs.


* **Persona 2 (Fatima Noor):** 22, Female, Bangalore, Student. Uses transit 4-5 times a week. Goal is to navigate routes easily. Pain point is anxiety with unclear directions.





# Scenario 2: Creating a Food Donation App Connecting Restaurants with NGOs



### 1. UX Problem Statements



* **User Pain Points:** Restaurants struggle to find NGOs, coordination is difficult, and there is a lack of feedback or confirmations.


* **Problem Statement 1:** *"A restaurant manager needs a way to quickly notify nearby NGOs about excess food because manual calls and emails are inefficient, which results in wasted food."*

* **Problem Statement 2:** *"An NGO volunteer needs a way to get reliable pickup confirmations because the current process lacks updates, which results in miscommunications and missed donations."*


### 2. Research Methods & Justification



* **Stakeholder Interviews:** To map out logistics and operational barriers. Exposes underlying trust issues.


* **Journey Mapping Workshops:** To visually map the food donation flow. Aligns different user perspectives.


* **Pilot Testing:** To validate the app in a real-world environment. Gives direct, actionable feedback.



### 3. Personas



* **Persona 1 (Rajiv Mehta):** 42, Male, Mumbai, Restaurant Owner. Uses WhatsApp for coordination. Goal is to reduce food waste. Pain point is having no confirmations.


* **Persona 2 (Sunita Reddy):** 33, Female, Hyderabad, NGO Coordinator. Uses spreadsheets and phone calls. Goal is to maximize food pickups. Pain point is the lack of real-time updates.



---

##  Result

Through this activity, clear UX problem frameworks, research strategies, and descriptive target personas were established for both scenarios. This foundational research directly addresses user anxiety and scheduling gaps in public transit, while eliminating manual communication and confirmation bottlenecks in the food donation workflow.
